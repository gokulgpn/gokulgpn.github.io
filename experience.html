Evaluation of Mode-Specific Retail Accessibility in Selected German Cities

Computing cumulative-opportunity accessibility to retail categories across 3–5 German cities using r5py (R5 routing engine) for all transport modes (car, bike, foot, public transport). Accessibility is modelled on a 100 m grid, aggregated to the 1 km Zensus 2022 census grid for comparison with socioeconomic indicators (income, unemployment, age, migration background, car ownership). Spatial patterns and inequalities are assessed through bivariate mapping and Spearman correlation. Results visualised in QGIS with maps, tables and figures.
