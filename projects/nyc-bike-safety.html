30DayMapChallenge Day 10 — North America: NYC Bike Safety

30DayMapChallengeNorth AmericaBike safetyKSINYCQGIS

Overview

Day 10 of the #30DayMapChallenge — theme: North America. This map highlights NYC neighbourhoods with high cyclist KSI (Killed or Seriously Injured) incidents and limited bike lanes, underscoring the urgent need for enhanced cycling infrastructure across the city.

This entry also marked the conclusion of the 2023 #30DayMapChallenge — 10 days of productive and enlightening map-making.

Methodology

Priority Area Data

Loaded the VZV_Bike Priority Areas dataset from the NYC Open Data Portal, identifying neighbourhoods with disproportionately high cyclist KSI rates and insufficient dedicated bike infrastructure.

Road Network

Integrated the road network from Geofabrik to provide spatial context for the priority areas and existing bike lane coverage.

Cartographic Output

Styled and composed the final map in QGIS, communicating where bicycle infrastructure expansion would have the strongest safety impact.

Data Sources

  • VZV_Bike Priority Areas — NYC Open Data Portal
  • Road Network — Geofabrik

Tools & Technologies

  • QGIS for processing, styling and map layout

Outcome

Produced a clear map of NYC's cyclist KSI hotspots, communicating where dedicated cycling infrastructure is most needed and bringing awareness to bike safety priorities across the city's boroughs.
